# ATTOM Unmapped Resume Fields Report

## Scope

This report compares the subject-property ATTOM data used in:

- `data/property-upserge/330_bird_st.json`
- `data/property-upserge/540_south_st.json`
- `data/property-upserge/1230_canby.json`

against the resume payloads seeded in:

- `scripts/seed-330-bird-st-project.ts`
- `scripts/seed-540-south-st-project.ts`
- `scripts/seed-1230-canby-rd-project.ts`

The comparison focuses on subject-property data from ATTOM endpoints such as `property_basicprofile`, `property_expandedprofile`, `property_detailowner`, and mortgage/detail variants when they describe the subject parcel.

For `540 South St`, the review also incorporated a supplemental lender-side Capitalize extract at `data/property-upserge/540-capitalize.txt` where it clarified current financing facts.

## What This Report Does Not Count

These ATTOM payload sections were intentionally excluded from the gap list:

- ZIP-level snapshot endpoints such as `property_address_zip`, `assessment_snapshot_zip`, `avm_snapshot_zip`, `sale_snapshot_geo_zip`, and sales-trend ZIP summaries.
- School endpoints.
- Sales comparables for neighboring properties.
- Request/response metadata such as ATTOM transaction IDs at the endpoint envelope level.

Those sections are available in the JSON files, but they do not represent the subject project or borrower resumes directly.

## Common Reasons A Field Was Left Unmapped

1. No direct resume schema field exists.
2. The field is public-record metadata, but the resume expects sponsor-provided underwriting input.
3. ATTOM provides conflicting values for the same concept.
4. ATTOM identifies owners, but not principal roles, guarantor status, ownership percentages, emails, or other borrower-profile details.
5. The value is a duplicate or lower-signal version of something already mapped in a cleaner form.

## Cross-Property Takeaways

1. The biggest unmapped buckets were geospatial metadata, public-record sale metadata, assessed-value breakdowns, and detailed ownership rosters.
2. The most important intentional omissions were the fields that looked structured but were not trustworthy enough to seed directly:

- `330 Bird`: beds/baths without explicit unit count.
- `540 South`: conflicting whole-building size figures.
- `1230 Canby`: direct conflict between `propertyType` and `unitsCount`.

1. Borrower-resume gaps were driven less by missing ATTOM data and more by ATTOM not telling us enough about sponsor roles. Owner names exist, but principal identity, role, email, biography, and ownership percentages do not.
2. If we want fuller mappings later, the best next step is a second-source layer that validates:

- actual unit counts and rentable area,
- sponsor/principal roster and ownership percentages,
- current business plan and requested loan terms,
- whether ATTOM legal/tax data should drive any additional structured fields.

1. The remaining resume backlog splits cleanly into two groups:

- ATTOM-adjacent fields that still need validation before mapping, such as unit count, average unit size, parking counts, and fuller SREO detail.
- Resume-native sponsor and underwriting fields that ATTOM cannot supply, such as principal bios, borrower contact info, sponsor net worth, deal terms, and operating performance.
