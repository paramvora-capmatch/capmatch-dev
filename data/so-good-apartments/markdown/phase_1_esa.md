## 1.0 EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Apex Environmental Solutions, Inc. (AES) was retained by Springfield Development Group, LLC (the "Client" or "User") to conduct a Phase I Environmental Site Assessment (ESA) for the property located at 123 Main Street, Springfield, [State] (the "Subject Property"). The purpose of this ESA is to identify recognized environmental conditions (RECs), controlled recognized environmental conditions (CRECs), and historical recognized environmental conditions (HRECs) in connection with the Subject Property, in accordance with the scope and limitations of ASTM International Standard Practice E1527-21, and to assist the User in satisfying the requirements for the "All Appropriate Inquiries" (AAI) defense to CERCLA liability.

The Subject Property comprises approximately 3.5 acres (152,460 square feet) and is currently occupied by a vacant single-story commercial structure and associated paved areas. The Client intends to redevelop the property into a $65,000,000 multi-family apartment complex, 'So-Good Apartments,' comprising 250 residential units and ancillary facilities.

Based on the investigation conducted for this Phase I ESA, which included a site reconnaissance, review of historical and regulatory records, and interviews, one (1) Recognized Environmental Condition (REC) has been identified in connection with the Subject Property:

*   **Undocumented Removal of Underground Storage Tanks (USTs) and Potential for Petroleum Impact:** Historical Sanborn Fire Insurance Maps (1948, 1960) and City Directories (1928-1965) indicate the Subject Property operated as "Springfield Auto Service," a full-service automotive repair and fueling station. These maps clearly depict two (2) USTs (each approximately 1,000-gallon capacity) situated at the eastern perimeter of the former service station building, and one (1) hydraulic lift within the service bay. A thorough review of state (e.g., [State] Department of Environmental Quality - SDEQ) and local (Springfield Fire Department) regulatory agency records revealed no documentation pertaining to the installation, operation, monitoring, removal, or closure of these identified USTs or the hydraulic lift. Despite visual inspection revealing no overt signs of petroleum staining, odors, or stressed vegetation during the site reconnaissance, the absence of proper closure documentation, coupled with the long operational history of a petroleum-dispensing facility, presents a material uncertainty regarding the environmental integrity of the subsurface in these specific areas. This condition constitutes a REC in accordance with ASTM E1527-21.

**Recommendations:**
Due to the identified REC, **Apex Environmental Solutions, Inc. recommends the initiation of a Phase II Environmental Site Assessment.** This Phase II ESA should include, at a minimum, the advancement of soil borings and collection of soil samples from within the suspected former UST basin areas and beneath the former hydraulic lift location. Samples should be analyzed for volatile organic compounds (VOCs), semi-volatile organic compounds (SVOCs), and total petroleum hydrocarbons (TPH) – gasoline range organics (GRO) and diesel range organics (DRO) – to ascertain the presence or absence of subsurface petroleum contamination. Should soil contamination be detected above applicable regulatory screening levels, further investigation, including groundwater assessment, may be warranted.

## 7.0 FINDINGS, OPINION, AND CONCLUSIONS

### 7.1 Recognized Environmental Conditions (RECs)

A **Recognized Environmental Condition (REC)** is defined by ASTM E1527-21 as the presence or likely presence of any hazardous substances or petroleum products in, on, or at a property: (1) due to any release to the environment; (2) under conditions indicative of a release to the environment; or (3) under conditions that pose a material threat of a future release to the environment.

Based on the information gathered during this Phase I ESA, one (1) REC has been identified in connection with the Subject Property:

*   **Undocumented Removal of Underground Storage Tanks (USTs) and Potential for Petroleum Impact:** Historical records (Sanborn Maps 1948 & 1960, City Directories 1928-1965) unequivocally indicate that the Subject Property operated as "Springfield Auto Service" from 1928 to 1965, a period during which two (2) USTs and a hydraulic lift were present. No records pertaining to the removal, closure, or environmental assessment of these USTs or the hydraulic lift were found in either state environmental agency files (SDEQ) or local fire department archives (SFD). While no overt surface indications of a release (e.g., staining, odors) were observed during site reconnaissance, the absence of proper closure documentation for petroleum-containing infrastructure historically present on the Subject Property constitutes a condition indicative of a potential release and, consequently, a material threat of future release, warranting further investigation.

### 7.2 Controlled Recognized Environmental Conditions (CRECs)

A **Controlled Recognized Environmental Condition (CREC)** is defined as a REC resulting from a past release of hazardous substances or petroleum products that has been addressed to the satisfaction of the applicable regulatory authority (e.g., a "no further action" letter) with hazardous substances or petroleum products allowed to remain in place subject to the implementation of activity and use limitations (AULs).

No CRECs were identified on the Subject Property.

### 7.3 Historical Recognized Environmental Conditions (HRECs)

A **Historical Recognized Environmental Condition (HREC)** is defined as a past REC that has been addressed to the satisfaction of the applicable regulatory authority without subjecting the property to AULs.

No HRECs were identified on the Subject Property. While the "Precision Components Inc." operation involved light manufacturing, no evidence was found to suggest releases occurred that required regulatory action or that any past RECs were fully remediated to current standards without AULs.

## 8.0 RECOMMENDATIONS

Given the identification of a Recognized Environmental Condition (REC) concerning the undocumented removal of USTs and a hydraulic lift associated with the former "Springfield Auto Service" operations, Apex Environmental Solutions, Inc. recommends the following:

1.  **Phase II Environmental Site Assessment:** Conduct a Phase II ESA to investigate potential subsurface contamination in the vicinity of the historically depicted USTs and hydraulic lift. This should include:
    *   Advancement of minimum three (3) soil borings targeting the former UST basin areas and one (1) boring at the former hydraulic lift location.
    *   Collection of discrete soil samples at varying depths (e.g., 5-foot intervals and at suspected tank bottom depths) from each boring.
    *   Analysis of selected soil samples for VOCs (specifically BTEX compounds), SVOCs (polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons - PAHs), and TPH (GRO and DRO) via EPA-approved methodologies.
    *   Contingent upon soil sample analytical results, and/or observation of groundwater during drilling, the collection and analysis of groundwater samples may be warranted.
2.  **Regulatory Notification:** Depending on the findings of the Phase II ESA, notification to the [State] Department of Environmental Quality (SDEQ) or other relevant regulatory agencies may be required.
