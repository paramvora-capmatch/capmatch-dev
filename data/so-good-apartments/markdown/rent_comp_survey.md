### 3. Methodology and Scope of Analysis

The methodology employed for this Survey involved a rigorous, multi-faceted approach to identify, analyze, and adjust comparable rental properties (the "Comps") within the immediate and extended Springfield submarket.

**A. Selection Criteria for Comparables:**

*   **Geographic Proximity:** Comps were primarily selected within a 2-mile radius of So-Good Apartments, with a maximum expansion to 3 miles for properties demonstrating exceptional relevance.
*   **Property Class:** Exclusively Class A multi-family properties, built post-2010 or extensively renovated post-2015, mirroring So-Good's premium quality and modern design.
*   **Amenity Parity:** Properties offering a similar depth and breadth of resident amenities, including fitness centers, communal spaces, and parking solutions.
*   **Unit Finishes & Build-Out:** Comparable interior finishes, including stainless steel appliances, quartz countertops, designer cabinetry, and in-unit washer/dryers.
*   **Management Quality:** Professionally managed properties with established reputations for resident services.
*   **Unit Mix & Size Distribution:** Properties with a comparable distribution of unit types and average unit sizes to facilitate accurate per-unit and per-square-foot comparisons.

**B. Data Collection and Verification:**

Data was sourced from a combination of proprietary subscription services (CoStar, Yardi Matrix, REIS), local real estate broker reports, direct on-site visits, public records (Springfield Assessor's Office), and anonymized lease agreements where available. Key data points collected for each comparable include: asking rents, effective rents (net of concessions), unit specifications (size, bedroom/bathroom count), occupancy rates, lease-up velocity (for newer properties), amenity packages, and property photographs. All data was cross-referenced for accuracy and timeliness, with a focus on active lease rates as of October 2023.

**C. Adjustment Protocol:**

Recognizing that no two properties are identical, a series of qualitative and quantitative adjustments were applied to the Comps' effective rents to normalize them against So-Good Apartments. These adjustments consider differences in:
*   Age and condition (favoring So-Good's new construction)
*   Amenity breadth and quality
*   Location attributes (e.g., walkability, transit access, views)
*   Unit finishes and technological integration
*   Lease concessions (e.g., free rent periods, reduced security deposits)

*   **Market Saturation:** While current submarket vacancy is low, a significant influx of new supply could temper rent growth or extend lease-up periods. Mitigated by So-Good's superior product differentiation and strategic amenity package.

*   **Lease-Up Risk:** Achieving projected occupancy and effective rents during the initial lease-up phase is paramount. Mitigated by robust pre-leasing efforts, competitive incentives, and a proven management team.

Based on the $65,000,000 project cost and typical debt-to-equity ratios for multi-family construction (e.g., 65-70% LTV), the projected NOI generated by these rents is anticipated to yield a DSCR comfortably above the lender's typical threshold of 1.25x - 1.35x, thereby affirming the financial viability of the proposed debt structure.
