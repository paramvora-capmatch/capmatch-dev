## Executive Summary

This Appraisal Summary Report presents a comprehensive review and underwriting opinion regarding the proposed multi-family development known as "So-Good Apartments," located at 1234 Prosperity Way in the burgeoning Midtown submarket of Springfield. The purpose of this report is to ascertain the market value of the fee simple absolute estate in the subject property, as stabilized, for financing and investment decision-making purposes.

Our rigorous analysis, encompassing the Cost Approach, Sales Comparison Approach, and Income Capitalization Approach (specifically Direct Capitalization and Discounted Cash Flow methodologies for a development-stage asset), supports a concluded Market Value of **Sixty-Five Million Dollars ($65,000,000)** as of the valuation date, October 20, 2023. This valuation is predicated upon the successful completion of construction, lease-up to stabilized occupancy, and adherence to the projected operating proforma. The development, designed as a Class A, 250-unit residential community, is strategically positioned to capitalize on robust demand drivers within the Springfield metropolitan statistical area (MSA).

## Property Identification and Description

The subject property, So-Good Apartments, comprises a proposed 250-unit, five-story, garden-style multi-family complex situated on a 2.5-acre parcel, legally described as Lot 17, Block 3, Prosperity Commons Subdivision, Springfield County. The project is currently in the late-stage entitlement phase, with site work anticipated to commence in Q1 2024. The development plan outlines a meticulously designed structure utilizing Type V-A construction, providing a gross building area of approximately 285,000 square feet, inclusive of common areas and a structured parking garage.

The unit mix will consist of:
*   **50 Studio Units:** Averaging 550 SF
*   **125 One-Bedroom Units:** Averaging 750 SF
*   **75 Two-Bedroom Units:** Averaging 1,050 SF

Luxury amenities are planned to include a state-of-the-art fitness center, co-working spaces, a resident lounge, a rooftop deck with city views, a resort-style swimming pool, and dedicated pet amenities. The project aims for LEED Silver certification, emphasizing sustainable building practices and energy efficiency. Parking will be provided at a ratio of 1.25 spaces per unit, primarily within a multi-level parking structure.

## Location and Market Analysis

Springfield’s Midtown submarket is characterized by its strategic location, offering excellent connectivity to the central business district (CBD), major employment hubs (e.g., 'TechInnovate Campus', 'Springfield Medical Corridor'), and cultural amenities. The submarket has demonstrated consistent demographic growth, with a 5-year average annual population increase of 1.8% and household income growth of 2.5%, significantly outpacing the national average.

The local multi-family market currently exhibits healthy fundamentals, with a Q3 2023 vacancy rate of 4.2% for Class A assets and an average effective rent growth of 6.1% year-over-year. Absorption rates remain strong, driven by a growing young professional demographic and increasing preference for rental housing over homeownership due to affordability constraints. While there is a pipeline of approximately 750 new units anticipated within the Springfield MSA over the next 18-24 months, the Midtown submarket specifically benefits from high barriers to entry, limiting competitive supply. So-Good Apartments' prime location, adjacent to the new "Riverfront Promenade" park and within walking distance to public transit, provides a distinct competitive advantage. The average market rent for comparable Class A properties in Midtown is approximately $2,150 per unit, with a range of $1,800 for studios to $2,800 for two-bedroom units.

## Valuation Methodology

### A. Cost Approach

The Cost Approach estimates value by summing the cost of the land and the depreciated cost of improvements. For So-Good Apartments, given its proposed new construction, the improvements are considered new and suffer no physical depreciation.

*   **Land Value:** Based on recent comparable land sales in the Midtown submarket, the 2.5-acre parcel is valued at approximately $7,500,000 ($3,000,000 per acre).
*   **Direct Construction Costs (Hard Costs):** Estimated at $185 per square foot (of gross building area), totaling $52,725,000. This includes all materials, labor, and contractor overhead/profit.
*   **Indirect Costs (Soft Costs):** Includes architectural and engineering fees (5% of hard costs), legal fees, permits, property taxes during construction, marketing, and interest carry during construction (estimated at 8.0% interest on average draw over 24-month construction period), totaling $7,800,000.
*   **Developer's Overhead & Profit:** Industry standard of 10% of total hard and soft costs, reflecting the entrepreneurial incentive and risk, totaling $6,052,500.

**Total Cost New (Rounded): $74,077,500**

*The Cost Approach provides a maximum value indication but often does not fully capture market demand or investor return expectations, particularly for income-producing assets.*

### B. Sales Comparison Approach

This approach analyzes recent sales of comparable properties to derive a value indication for the subject. Given that So-Good Apartments is a proposed development, direct comparisons to *stabilized* new Class A multi-family sales are more pertinent for deriving a *stabilized* market value. We identified five highly comparable transactions in the Springfield MSA over the past 12 months, with three located within the Midtown submarket.

Key comparable sales analysis:
*   **"The Apex Residences"**: Sold 6 months ago, 220 units, Class A, similar amenities, $61,500,000 ($279,545/unit). Stabilized occupancy 94%, Cap Rate 4.95%.
*   **"Riverbend Lofts"**: Sold 9 months ago, 180 units, Class A, superior location (riverfront), $54,000,000 ($300,000/unit). Stabilized occupancy 96%, Cap Rate 4.70%.
*   **"Uptown Flats"**: Sold 4 months ago, 280 units, Class A, slightly inferior amenities, $72,800,000 ($260,000/unit). Stabilized occupancy 93%, Cap Rate 5.10%.

After applying adjustments for location, age, quality of construction, amenities, parking, unit mix, and time of sale, the adjusted range per unit for the subject property falls between $265,000 and $285,000. Averaging these adjusted comparable sales data suggests an implied value per unit of approximately $272,000.

**Indicated Value (250 units x $272,000/unit): $68,000,000**

*This approach is particularly strong for a stabilized asset, but requires careful adjustments for the subject's development status.*

### C. Income Capitalization Approach

This approach, paramount for income-producing properties, involves two methodologies: Direct Capitalization and Discounted Cash Flow (DCF).

#### 1. Direct Capitalization (Stabilized Value)

This method converts a single year's net operating income (NOI) into value using an overall capitalization rate (cap rate).

*   **Potential Gross Income (PGI) - Stabilized:**
    *   Studio: 50 units * $1,800/month = $90,000/month
    *   1-Bedroom: 125 units * $2,100/month = $262,500/month
    *   2-Bedroom: 75 units * $2,500/month = $187,500/month
    *   Monthly PGI: $540,000
    *   Annual PGI: $6,480,000
*   **Other Income:** (Parking, storage, pet fees, amenities) - Estimated at $300 per unit annually = $75,000
*   **Total Annual PGI + Other Income:** $6,555,000
*   **Vacancy & Collection Loss:** Market vacancy of 5.0% = $327,750
*   **Effective Gross Income (EGI):** $6,227,250
*   **Operating Expenses:** (Estimated based on comparable Class A properties in Midtown, including property taxes, insurance, utilities, management fee (4.0% of EGI), repairs & maintenance, administrative, reserves for replacement ($300/unit/year)). Total annual operating expenses: $1,432,260 (23% of EGI).
*   **Net Operating Income (NOI):** $4,794,990

Market-derived capitalization rates for stabilized, Class A multi-family assets in the Midtown submarket range from 4.75% to 5.25%. Given the superior quality and location of So-Good Apartments, a cap rate of **4.90%** is deemed appropriate.

**Indicated Value (NOI / Cap Rate): $4,794,990 / 0.0490 = $97,856,938**

*This high value reflects a fully stabilized, income-producing asset. For a development project, the Discounted Cash Flow is more appropriate for reflecting the present value.*

#### 2. Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) Analysis

The DCF projects annual cash flows over a typical investment horizon (e.g., 10 years for multi-family), including construction costs, lease-up phase, stabilized operations, and a terminal value, then discounts these cash flows back to the present. This methodology inherently accounts for the time value of money and the risks associated with development and lease-up.

Key assumptions for DCF model:
*   **Construction Period:** 24 months, with expenditures financed through phased draws.
*   **Lease-Up Period:** 12 months post-completion, achieving 93% occupancy.
*   **Rental Growth:** 3.0% annually after stabilization.
*   **Expense Growth:** 2.5% annually.
*   **Terminal Cap Rate:** 5.00% (applied to Year 11 NOI to calculate terminal value).
*   **Discount Rate (IRR):** An unlevered discount rate of 7.50% is utilized, reflecting the perceived risk of a Class A development project in this market. This rate incorporates a risk premium over the long-term risk-free rate.

The DCF model projects total revenues of approximately $6.2M in Year 3 (first full stabilized year) growing to $7.8M by Year 10. Total operating expenses escalate from $1.4M to $1.8M over the same period. The terminal value, calculated by capitalizing Year 11 NOI, adds significant weight.

**Present Value of Cash Flows (DCF): $65,500,000**

*This approach, explicitly accounting for development and lease-up risk through the discount rate and phased cash flows, provides the most robust indication of value for a project in this stage.*

## Reconciliation and Final Value Conclusion

The three valuation approaches provide a range of values:
*   Cost Approach: $74,077,500
*   Sales Comparison Approach: $68,000,000
*   Income Capitalization Approach (DCF): $65,500,000
*   Income Capitalization Approach (Direct Cap, Stabilized): $97,856,938 (This reflects fully built and operating, which is not the present condition)

In reconciling these indications, the Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) analysis within the Income Capitalization Approach is given the most significant weight (70%), as it directly incorporates the inherent risks and costs associated with new development, construction, and lease-up. The Sales Comparison Approach is given secondary weight (20%), providing market-derived pricing for similar *stabilized* assets, which is then adjusted for the subject's non-stabilized status implicitly within the DCF's discount rate. The Cost Approach is given minimal weight (10%) as it represents the "cost to create" and may not fully align with market investor expectations for an income-generating asset.

Based on this reconciliation, the final Market Value of the fee simple absolute interest in So-Good Apartments, as of October 20, 2023, subject to the assumptions and limiting conditions herein, is concluded to be:

**Sixty-Five Million Dollars ($65,000,000)**

## Underwriter's Risk Assessment

As a senior commercial real estate underwriter, my assessment identifies several key risks associated with the So-Good Apartments development:

*   **Construction Risk:** Potential for cost overruns (e.g., due to material price volatility, labor shortages) and schedule delays. Mitigated by an experienced general contractor and robust contingency budgeting (10% of hard costs).
*   **Lease-Up Risk:** While market fundamentals are strong, prolonged lease-up periods or inability to achieve proforma rents could impact cash flow and debt service coverage. Mitigation: Aggressive pre-leasing strategy, competitive amenities, and a strong property management team.
*   **Market Saturation Risk:** Increased supply in the broader MSA, though limited in Midtown, could exert downward pressure on rents and occupancy in the long term. Continuous monitoring of competitive supply is advised.
*   **Interest Rate Risk:** Significant increases in interest rates during the construction or stabilization phase could impact refinance opportunities or future capital costs.
*   **Operational Risk:** Post-stabilization, effective property management will be crucial for maintaining high occupancy, controlling expenses, and ensuring tenant satisfaction.
