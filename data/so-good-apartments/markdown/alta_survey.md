This section details the specific findings and observations made during the field survey, addressing the requirements of the selected Table A items.

*   All property corners for the perimeter boundary were found or set as described in the legal description and General Notes. No new monuments were required to be set at corners.

**2. Address:**
*   The property's physical address is 1234 Prosperity Boulevard, Springfield, Capital County, Grandia. This address is prominently displayed at the primary ingress point.

**3. Flood Zone Designation:**
*   Based on the Federal Emergency Management Agency (FEMA) Flood Insurance Rate Map (FIRM) Panel No. 17019C0345F, effective date March 15, 2020, the entire subject property lies within **Zone X**, indicating areas outside the 100-year floodplain. No portion of the site is within a Special Flood Hazard Area (SFHA).

**4. Gross Land Area:**
*   The gross surveyed area of the property, calculated from the record legal description and field measurements, is **8.75 acres (381,150 square feet)**, more or less.

**5. Zoning Classification:**
*   The subject property is zoned **R-3 (High-Density Residential)** by the City of Springfield Planning Department. Key zoning requirements applicable to the development include:
    *   **Minimum Lot Area:** 1.0 acre. (Site: 8.75 acres – Complies)
    *   **Maximum Height:** 75 feet or 6 stories. (Proposed 4-story buildings – Complies)
    *   **Minimum Setbacks:**
        *   Front (Prosperity Blvd): 25 feet. (Buildings set back 45 feet – Complies)
        *   Side (East & West): 15 feet. (Buildings set back 30 feet – Complies)

    *   **Minimum Parking:** 1.5 spaces per dwelling unit. (See Table A Item 11)

    *   **(a) Visible Evidence of Easements:**
        *   A 15-foot wide overhead electric transmission line easement runs along the eastern boundary of the property, serving adjacent parcels. Power poles are visible and within the easement. (No current or proposed buildings are within this easement).
        *   A 20-foot wide underground sanitary sewer easement is visible crossing the southern portion of the property, parallel to Prosperity Boulevard. Multiple manholes are visible. (Building 1 is constructed clear of this easement by 10 feet).
        *   A storm drainage swale and associated 10-foot wide easement is visible along the western property line, draining into the municipal storm system.
    *   **(b) Easements, Encroachments, and Restrictions of Record:**
        *   **Utility Easement (Sewer):** A 20-foot wide sanitary sewer easement, as recorded in Document No. 2005-00123456, Official Records of Capital County, burdens the southern portion of the property, located 10 feet north of the northern edge of the Prosperity Boulevard right-of-way. No permanent improvements of the So-Good Apartments encroach into this easement.
        *   **Utility Easement (Electric):** A 15-foot wide overhead electric transmission line easement, as recorded in Document No. 1998-00078901, Official Records of Capital County, runs parallel to and along the entire eastern boundary of the property. No structures or improvements related to the So-Good Apartments are located within this easement, except for perimeter landscaping.
        *   **Access and Reciprocal Parking Easement:** A 30-foot wide ingress/egress and reciprocal parking easement, recorded in Document No. 2018-00030030, benefits the adjacent commercial parcel to the west. The So-Good Apartments development respects this easement and has incorporated it into its site plan.

This is a pre-existing condition and does not impact the proposed development. This minor encroachment has been reported to the title insurer.
