# Construction Schedule: So-Good Apartments Development

## Document Preamble and Project Identification

This document, hereinafter referred to as the "Construction Schedule" or "Schedule," details the critical temporal parameters and logistical sequencing for the development of the multi-family residential project known as **"So-Good Apartments."** This Schedule is issued by **Springfield Development Partners, LLC** (hereinafter, the "Developer"), and shall govern the performance of **Apex Constructors, Inc.** (hereinafter, the "General Contractor" or "GC"), and all their respective sub-contractors, suppliers, and consultants.

The project is located at **123 Grand Avenue, Springfield, [State, e.g., IL]**, and comprises the ground-up construction of a 280-unit, five-story apartment complex featuring structured parking, a full amenity package, and associated site improvements. The total project budget is approximately **$65,000,000.00**, inclusive of land acquisition, hard costs, soft costs, and owner's contingency. This Schedule constitutes an integral and binding component of the Development Agreement and the General Construction Contract executed on **December 15, 2023**, and is subject to the terms and conditions stipulated therein. **Time is of the essence** in the execution of all phases and milestones outlined herein.

## Project Overview and Scope of Work Summary

The So-Good Apartments development entails the construction of a Type III-A wood-frame structure over a Type I-A concrete podium, encompassing approximately 320,000 gross square feet, including 220,000 residential net rentable square feet and 60,000 square feet of structured parking. The scope includes, but is not limited to, comprehensive site preparation, utility infrastructure, foundation systems, structural framing, building enclosure, extensive mechanical, electrical, plumbing, and fire protection (MEP/FP) systems, high-quality interior finishes, sophisticated exterior façade systems, full landscaping, and commissioning of all building systems.

The project is financed through a senior construction loan facility provided by **First Capital Bank**, with a total hard cost budget allocated at **$45,000,000.00**. Payment draw requests will be strictly tied to the verified achievement of the milestones delineated in this Schedule, as certified by the Lender's independent construction monitor.

## Key Project Milestones and Critical Dates

The following represent the overarching critical milestones for the So-Good Apartments development. Adherence to these dates is paramount, and any anticipated deviation must be formally communicated and mitigated in accordance with the contract terms.

*   **Project Commencement (Notice to Proceed):** January 1, 2024
*   **Completion of Major Permitting & Approvals:** March 31, 2024
*   **Substantial Completion of Site Work & Foundations:** July 31, 2024
*   **Topping Out (Structural Frame Complete):** January 31, 2025
*   **Building Enclosure Weathertight:** April 30, 2025
*   **Rough-In Completion (MEP/FP):** June 30, 2025
*   **Substantial Completion (Certificate of Substantial Completion):** December 15, 2025
*   **Final Certificate of Occupancy (C of O):** January 15, 2026
*   **Final Completion & Project Closeout:** January 31, 2026

Failure to achieve the Substantial Completion milestone by December 15, 2025, may trigger the assessment of **liquidated damages** at a rate of **$5,000.00 per day**, as stipulated in Article 14 of the General Construction Contract.

## Detailed Construction Phasing

The following detailed phasing plan outlines the sequential progression of work, inclusive of estimated durations, critical dependencies, and allocated hard cost percentages. All durations are presented in calendar days unless otherwise specified.

### Phase 1: Pre-Construction & Permitting (January 1, 2024 – March 31, 2024)
*   **Duration:** 90 Days
*   **Hard Cost Allocation:** 0% (soft costs include A&E, permit fees of approximately $350,000)
*   **Key Activities:**
    *   **January 1 - January 31, 2024:** Finalization of Construction Documents (CDs) across all disciplines (Architectural, Structural, Civil, MEP/FP, Landscape). Coordination of value engineering efforts.
    *   **January 15 - February 29, 2024:** Final Permit Submissions to the City of Springfield Department of Planning and Development, Department of Public Works, and relevant utility authorities (e.g., electric, gas, water, sanitary sewer).
    *   **February 1 - March 15, 2024:** Procurement and execution of major subcontracts (e.g., excavation, concrete, structural steel, framing).
    *   **March 1 - March 31, 2024:** Establishment of project-specific safety plans, quality assurance/quality control (QA/QC) protocols, and site logistics planning. Baseline Critical Path Method (CPM) schedule development and approval.
*   **Critical Dependencies:** Timely issuance of all required building permits, zoning approvals, and environmental clearances.

### Phase 2: Site Work & Foundations (April 1, 2024 – July 31, 2024)
*   **Duration:** 122 Days
*   **Hard Cost Allocation:** 10% (**$4,500,000.00**)
*   **Key Activities:**
    *   **April 1 - April 30, 2024:** Site mobilization, establishment of erosion control measures, preliminary clearing and grubbing. Demolition of existing minor structures (if any) and utility disconnections.
    *   **May 1 - May 31, 2024:** Earthwork operations including mass excavation to design subgrade, cut/fill operations, and rough grading. Installation of underground storm drainage infrastructure.
    *   **June 1 - June 30, 2024:** Installation of underground sanitary sewer and water supply lines. Compaction testing and subgrade preparation for foundations.
    *   **July 1 - July 31, 2024:** Pouring of spread footings, foundation walls, and the concrete podium slab. Installation of below-grade waterproofing and damp proofing systems.
*   **Critical Dependencies:** Geo-technical reports, utility availability, and weather conditions.

### Phase 3: Structural Framing (August 1, 2024 – January 31, 2025)
*   **Duration:** 184 Days
*   **Hard Cost Allocation:** 20% (**$9,000,000.00**)
*   **Key Activities:**
    *   **August 1 - October 31, 2024:** Erection of concrete columns and walls for the podium level. Installation of elevated concrete decks (Levels 2 and 3 parking).
    *   **November 1, 2024 - January 31, 2025:** Installation of wood framing for residential levels (Levels 2-5). This includes bearing walls, floor joists, roof trusses, and sheathing. Vertical transportation shafts (elevator, stairwells) framing.
*   **Critical Dependencies:** Concrete cure times, lumber delivery schedules, and crane availability. Early installation of vertical components (e.g., stair towers) is critical for early access for follow-on trades.

### Phase 4: Building Enclosure (November 1, 2024 – April 30, 2025)
*   **Duration:** 181 Days (Overlaps with Structural Framing)
*   **Hard Cost Allocation:** 15% (**$6,750,000.00**)
*   **Key Activities:**
    *   **November 1, 2024 - January 31, 2025:** Roof deck installation, parapet construction, and initial roofing membrane application to achieve weather-tight conditions for the structure.
    *   **February 1 - March 31, 2025:** Installation of exterior wall sheathing, continuous insulation, and weather-resistive barrier (WRB).
    *   **April 1 - April 30, 2025:** Installation of windows and exterior doors. Finalization of the temporary building enclosure to protect interior work.
*   **Critical Dependencies:** Achievement of weathertight condition is prerequisite for interior rough-ins.

### Phase 5: Rough-Ins (MEP/FP/Low Voltage) (January 1, 2025 – June 30, 2025)
*   **Duration:** 181 Days (Heavy Overlap with Enclosure and early Finishes)
*   **Hard Cost Allocation:** 25% (**$11,250,000.00**)
*   **Key Activities:**
    *   **January 1 - March 31, 2025:** Installation of electrical conduits, wiring, and junction boxes. Plumbing rough-ins (water supply, waste, vent lines) and fire protection piping.
    *   **April 1 - May 31, 2025:** Installation of HVAC ductwork, ventilation systems, and primary equipment (e.g., package units, chillers). Low voltage rough-ins for data, security, and telecommunications.
    *   **June 1 - June 30, 2025:** Final connections for all rough-in systems, including pressure testing of plumbing and fire lines, and initial circuit continuity tests for electrical.
*   **Critical Dependencies:** Coordination of trades in confined spaces. Requires a fully framed and substantially enclosed building.

### Phase 6: Interior Finishes (April 1, 2025 – October 31, 2025)
*   **Duration:** 214 Days (Heavy Overlap with Rough-Ins and Exterior Finishes)
*   **Hard Cost Allocation:** 25% (**$11,250,000.00**)
*   **Key Activities:**
    *   **April 1 - May 31, 2025:** Insulation installation, drywall hanging and finishing (Levels 2-5).
    *   **June 1 - July 31, 2025:** Interior door frame and door installation. Millwork and cabinetry installation. Primer application and initial paint coats.
    *   **August 1 - September 30, 2025:** Flooring installation (e.g., LVT, carpet tile, ceramic tile). Installation of plumbing fixtures, electrical devices, and lighting fixtures.
    *   **October 1 - October 31, 2025:** Appliance installation, final paint coats, installation of mirrors and finish hardware.
*   **Critical Dependencies:** Drywall completion and proper climate control (HVAC) are essential before finish trades.

### Phase 7: Exterior Finishes & Landscaping (September 1, 2025 – November 30, 2025)
*   **Duration:** 91 Days (Heavy Overlap with Interior Finishes)
*   **Hard Cost Allocation:** 5% (**$2,250,000.00**)
*   **Key Activities:**
    *   **September 1 - October 31, 2025:** Installation of exterior cladding (e.g., brick veneer, fiber cement siding, stucco systems). Balcony railing installation.
    *   **October 15 - November 30, 2025:** Hardscaping (concrete sidewalks, unit paver systems, curbs, asphalt paving for surface parking and drives). Installation of site lighting and signage.
    *   **November 1 - November 30, 2025:** Softscaping (planting of trees, shrubs, ground cover, and hydroseeding/sodding). Installation of irrigation system.
*   **Critical Dependencies:** Weather conditions significantly impact exterior work. Completion of exterior cladding is required for final utility connections in some cases.

### Phase 8: Final Inspections & Commissioning (November 1, 2025 – December 31, 2025)
*   **Duration:** 61 Days (Overlaps with late interior finishes and punch list)
*   **Hard Cost Allocation:** 0% (Covered by GC Overhead & Profit)
*   **Key Activities:**
    *   **November 1 - November 30, 2025:** Comprehensive testing and balancing (TAB) of HVAC systems. Functional testing of all electrical panels, lighting controls, and low voltage systems.
    *   **December 1 - December 15, 2025:** Pre-final inspections by the Developer's team, third-party consultants (e.g., structural, envelope), and jurisdictional authorities (e.g., Building Department, Fire Marshal, Health Department).
    *   **December 16 - December 31, 2025:** Remediation of any identified deficiencies from pre-final inspections. Preparation for Certificate of Substantial Completion application.
*   **Critical Dependencies:** All systems must be fully installed and operational for testing.

### Phase 9: Punch List & Project Closeout (December 1, 2025 – January 31, 2026)
*   **Duration:** 62 Days (Overlaps with inspections)
*   **Hard Cost Allocation:** 0% (Covered by GC Overhead & Profit)
*   **Key Activities:**
    *   **December 1 - December 31, 2025:** Generation of comprehensive punch lists by the Developer, Architect, and GC. Active remediation of punch list items.
    *   **January 1 - January 15, 2026:** Final cleaning of all residential units and common areas. Final inspections by all governing authorities leading to issuance of the **Final Certificate of Occupancy**.
    *   **January 16 - January 31, 2026:** Submission of as-built drawings, operations & maintenance manuals, warranty documentation, lien waivers, and final closeout documentation to the Developer and Lender. Resolution of all outstanding financial obligations.
*   **Critical Dependencies:** Complete remediation of all life-safety and code-related punch list items for C of O.

## Critical Path Method (CPM) and Contingency

This Schedule has been developed leveraging the Critical Path Method (CPM) to identify activities whose delay will directly impact the overall project completion date. The structural framing, building enclosure, and rough-in phases have been identified as segments with minimal float and are subject to continuous monitoring via bi-weekly look-ahead schedules.

A **10% contingency** on remaining hard costs will be maintained by the Developer and Lender to address unforeseen conditions, scope changes, or schedule impacts that may arise. The GC shall maintain a separate contingency for self-performance and subcontractor risk mitigation. Requests for utilization of owner's contingency shall require formal Change Order authorization and Lender approval.

## Reporting and Communication Protocols

The General Contractor shall provide the Developer and Lender with a detailed project progress report every two weeks, inclusive of:
*   Updated CPM schedule highlighting actual vs. planned progress.
*   Percentage completion for each scheduled activity and overall project.
*   Detailed RFI (Request for Information) and Submittal logs, with status updates.
*   Change Order status log.
*   Safety incident reports.
*   Three-week look-ahead schedule.
*   Photographic documentation of critical progress.

Weekly progress meetings shall be held on-site every Tuesday at 9:00 AM, attended by representatives from the Developer, GC, Architect, and key subcontractors. Monthly owner's meetings shall incorporate Lender representation for financial and schedule review.

## Force Majeure Clause

Performance under this Construction Schedule shall be subject to the Force Majeure provisions as set forth in Article 17 of the General Construction Contract. Events beyond the reasonable control of the performing party, including but not limited to acts of God, war, terrorism, civil unrest, labor disputes, epidemics, pandemics, or unforeseen governmental actions, may justify a commensurate extension of the project timeline, provided that the affected party provides prompt written notice and exercises commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the delay. Monetary compensation for such events shall be governed by the specific terms of the Contract.
